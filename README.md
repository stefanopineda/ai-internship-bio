## Frameworks I Think With

**The Value Equation (Alex Hormozi)** — my guiding light when evaluating any product idea:

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

Maximize the top, minimize the bottom. I've watched hundreds of hours of Hormozi breaking down businesses and can get ~80% of the way to that kind of teardown for a student's idea.

**The Dhandho Principle (Mohnish Pabrai)** — "heads I win, tails I don't lose much." Low risk, high uncertainty. One I can talk about at length and apply to choosing which bets to make.

**The 80/20 Principle (Richard Koch)** — a small share of inputs drives most of the outputs. I genuinely run my life and work on this.

**Incentives drive behavior and outcomes** — you can measure how good something is by the outcomes it produces; where outcomes are bad, the incentives are misaligned. I've seen this firsthand managing rental properties and navigating the medical field around my daughter's recent birth — recognizing where a doctor's incentives weren't aligned with ours and pushing back.

**Antifragility & risk (Nassim Taleb)** — making decisions under uncertainty, surviving the downside, and positioning for asymmetric upside.
