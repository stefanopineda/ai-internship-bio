# The Only Equation in Business That Matters
### Why People Buy — and How to Make Them Want To

**Format:** ~60 min (45 min talk + 15 min live product teardowns)
**One-liner:** "Value" isn't a vibe. It's a fraction. Once you can see the fraction, you can engineer demand for anything.

---

## Hook (3 min)
- I've watched *hundreds of hours* of Alex Hormozi breaking down businesses. I can't do it as well as he can — but I can get maybe 80% of the way there, and 80% is more than enough to evaluate your own idea or critique a company's marketing.
- This is the single framework I hold up as a guiding light whenever I think about a product I might build. If a product idea doesn't survive this equation, I don't build it.

## Part 1 — The Equation (10 min)
From Hormozi's *$100M Offers*:

> **Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)**

Four levers. To create value you push the top up and the bottom down:
- **Dream Outcome (↑)** — how badly does the customer want the result? Sell the destination, not the vehicle.
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (↑)** — do they believe *it'll work for them*? Guarantees, proof, testimonials, track record. This is usually the cheapest lever to move and the most neglected.
- **Time Delay (↓)** — how fast do they get the result? Faster = more valuable. "Lose weight in 90 days" beats "in 2 years."
- **Effort & Sacrifice (↓)** — how much work/pain/risk for the customer? Done-for-you beats do-it-yourself.

**Key insight:** most businesses compete only on Dream Outcome (a better promise). The leverage is in the other three — *believability, speed, and ease*. That's where you win.

## Part 2 — Live Teardowns (18 min)
Pick 3–4 goods/services and run them through the equation out loud with the room. For each: rate the four levers, find the weak one, fix it.

- **A SaaS product** (e.g., a scheduling tool): Dream = "never lose a booking." Likelihood lever = onboarding + integrations + social proof. Time delay = setup friction (kill it). Effort = automation. *Marketing pathway:* free trial, templates, "set up in 5 minutes."
- **An automation / AI service** (my lane): Dream = "your tedious work disappears." The whole pitch is collapsing Time Delay and Effort to near zero — that's why automation sells. *Marketing pathway:* before/after, hours saved, ROI calculator.
- **A physical good** (e.g., a mattress or a supplement): Dream = better sleep/health. Likelihood = reviews, trial period, money-back guarantee (directly attacks perceived risk). *Marketing pathway:* 100-night trial = removing effort/risk from the buyer.
- **A high-ticket service** (e.g., consulting/coaching): Dream = transformation. Done-for-you crushes Effort & Sacrifice, which is why it commands premium pricing.

For each, ask the room: **"What does this company's marketing actually focus on? Which lever are they selling?"** Often you'll find the marketing emphasizes the wrong lever.

## Part 3 — Marketing Pathways: What Actually Sells (8 min)
- A great offer fails with no traffic; great traffic fails with a weak offer. The equation builds the *offer*; the four lead lanes (warm/cold × one-to-one/one-to-many, from the sales session) deliver the *traffic*.
- **What actually sells:** the lever the customer is most anxious about. Skeptical market → sell *likelihood* (proof, guarantee). Impatient market → sell *speed*. Lazy/busy market → sell *ease* (done-for-you). Aspirational market → sell *dream*.
- *$100M Leads* + *Hacking Growth (Sean Ellis)*: find your one growth lever, instrument it, double down. Don't diversify channels until one is saturated.
- *The Ultimate Sales Machine (Holmes)*: pigheaded discipline on the few fundamentals beats chasing every new tactic.

## Part 4 — How I Use This On My Own Startup (4 min)
- My AI receptionist business, run through the equation live: Dream = "never miss a customer call." Likelihood = real demos + trial. Time delay = live in a day. Effort = we set it up for you. Where it's weak and what I'm fixing right now.

## Close + Exercise (15 min)
- **Exercise:** every student writes their idea (or a product they love) as the four-lever fraction, identifies the weakest lever, and proposes one fix.
- Rapid-fire: I score a few out loud.
