# I Knocked on 500 Doors So You Don't Have To
### Selling Anything, From Doorbells to DMs

**Format:** ~60 min (45 min talk + 15 min Q&A / role-play)
**One-liner:** Everyone is always selling something. The medium changed; the human being on the other side didn't.

---

## Hook (3 min)
- Two months on shore between submarine duties, I voluntarily did door-to-door sales for Verizon. Not because I needed the money — because I wanted reps. I wanted to know what it felt like to hear "no" 50 times in an afternoon and ring the next doorbell anyway.
- The single most underrated entrepreneurship skill is sales. Most technical founders can build anything and sell nothing. If you can sell, you will never starve.
- Today: the history of selling, the modern playbook, and the psychology that has never changed.

## Part 1 — The Evolution of the Sale (8 min)
Walk the timeline; the *channel* moved, the *principles* didn't:
1. **In-person / marketplace** — the bazaar, the general store, Sam Walton stocking shelves and watching customers. *Made in America (Sam Walton):* relentless customer obsession, learning by walking the floor.
2. **Door-to-door** — my Verizon stint. You are interrupting someone's dinner. You have 7 seconds. You learn that tonality, posture, and the first sentence are everything.
3. **Phone sales** — the cold call. Volume, scripts, objection handling. I do this *today* cold-calling small businesses for my AI receptionist startup.
4. **Digital sales** — landing pages, email, DMs, ads. The doorbell became a "Notify me" button. You can now knock on 10,000 doors while you sleep.
- **Takeaway:** every era rewards the same thing — earning attention and reducing the friction to "yes."

## Part 2 — How You Actually Take Money and Measure Demand (8 min)
Practical, do-it-this-week mechanics:
- **Accept payments before you build anything** — Stripe/Stripe Checkout, a simple link. If people won't pay, you don't have a business, you have a hobby.
- **The "first to find out" opt-in** — a one-page site with an email capture: *"Be the first to know when it goes live."* This is the cheapest market-demand test on earth. Real emails > polite "I'd totally buy that."
- **Pre-sells beat surveys** — a pre-order or a deposit is the only honest survey.
- Tie to *Ready, Fire, Aim (Masterson):* get something in front of a customer fast, then refine. Selling is how you learn what to build.

## Part 3 — The Hormozi Map: Four Ways to Get Customers (10 min)
Lead generation has exactly four lanes (from Hormozi's *$100M Leads*):

| | **Warm** (people who know you) | **Cold** (strangers) |
|---|---|---|
| **One-to-one** | Warm outreach (texts, calls to your network) | Cold outreach (cold calls, cold DMs, cold email) |
| **One-to-many** | Posting free content to your audience | Paid ads to strangers |

- **Warm outreach** — start here, it's free and converts best. The "100-person list."
- **Cold outreach** — my current daily reality. Volume × personalization × follow-up. Most money is in the follow-up.
- **Content (one-to-many warm)** — compounding leverage; I made a YouTube video that sells my AI work while I sleep.
- **Paid ads** — buying attention; only do it once the offer converts organically.
- **Takeaway:** pick ONE lane and saturate it before adding a second.

## Part 4 — Influence & Persuasion: The Unchanging Psychology (12 min)
The channel changes; the brain doesn't. Cialdini's six (from *Influence / Persuasion*):
1. **Reciprocity** — give first (free value, a useful teardown).
2. **Commitment & consistency** — small yes leads to big yes (the opt-in is a small yes).
3. **Social proof** — testimonials, "join 400 others."
4. **Authority** — credentials, demonstrated expertise (be the obvious expert).
5. **Liking** — people buy from people they like; similarity and genuine interest.
6. **Scarcity** — real deadlines and limited slots.

Then tie it to character, because tactics without trust is manipulation:
- **It all stems from *7 Habits (Covey)*** — "seek first to understand, then to be understood" (Habit 5) is the real secret of every great closer. The best salespeople ask more than they pitch.
- **"Think win/win" (Habit 4)** — the sale that screws the customer kills the next ten referrals.
- The closing classics reinforce this: *Secrets of Closing the Sale (Ziglar)* — you can have everything in life you want if you help enough others get what they want; *How I Raised Myself from Failure to Success in Selling (Bettger)* — enthusiasm and asking questions; *The Psychology of Selling (Tracy)* — 80% of selling is psychology and self-image; *The Ultimate Sales Machine (Holmes)* — pigheaded discipline on a few fundamentals beats a hundred tactics.

## Part 5 — My Reps, Unfiltered (4 min)
- Verizon doorstep: the rejections that taught me detachment from outcome.
- Navy recruiting: selling the hardest "product" there is — a multi-year commitment to a teenager's parents.
- Cold-calling for my own startup *right now*: what's working, what isn't, live.

## Close + Exercise (15 min, interactive)
- **Live role-play:** a student pitches me their (real or made-up) product in 30 seconds. I object. We fix it together using the frameworks.
- **Homework:** put up a one-page "notify me" opt-in for any idea this week and send 10 warm-outreach messages.
