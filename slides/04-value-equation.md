### Part 1
# The equation

<!--
From Hormozi's $100M Offers. We're going to build the fraction piece by piece.
Don't write it down yet — just watch it assemble. By the end of this part you'll never un-see it.
-->

---

## Straight from the source.

> *$100M Offers* — **Alex Hormozi**

<!--
Full credit: this equation is Alex Hormozi's, from his book $100M Offers. I didn't invent it; I just live by it.
If you take one book away from today, it's this one — it's short, blunt, and it changed how I see every business.
We're going to assemble his fraction one term at a time.
-->

---

<!-- _class: statement -->

> Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

<!--
Here it is. The full equation from Alex Hormozi's $100M Offers.
Read it out loud slowly. Two things on top you want to be BIG. Two things on the bottom you want to be SMALL.
To create value you push the top up and the bottom down. That's the entire game. Everything else today is detail.
-->

---

## Four levers. That's it.

- **Dream Outcome** ↑
- **Perceived Likelihood** ↑
- **Time Delay** ↓
- **Effort & Sacrifice** ↓

<!--
Four levers. Two you maximize, two you minimize.
Memorize these four words. Every offer you ever evaluate gets scored on exactly these.
We'll take each one in turn and I'll show you which ones businesses obsess over and which they ignore.
-->

---

<!-- _class: divider -->

### Lever 1 — push it up
# Dream Outcome ↑

<!--
How badly does the customer want the result?
This is the promise — the destination the customer is buying their way toward.
-->

---

## Dream Outcome ↑

**Sell the destination, not the vehicle.**

<!--
How badly does the customer want the result? The bigger and more vivid the dream, the more value.
Sell the destination, not the vehicle. Nobody wants a drill; they want the hole — and really, the shelf, and the tidy home.
This is the lever everyone instinctively reaches for: make a bigger, bolder promise. Hold that thought — because in a minute I'll show you it's the WRONG place to compete.
-->

---

<!-- _class: divider -->

### Lever 2 — push it up
# Perceived Likelihood ↑

<!--
Do they believe it'll actually work FOR THEM?
This is the lever almost nobody pulls — and it's the cheapest one to move.
-->

---

## Perceived Likelihood ↑

Do they believe **it'll work for them?**

*Guarantees · proof · testimonials · track record.*

<!--
The dream is great, but do they believe THEY specifically will get it? That's perceived likelihood of achievement.
You move it with guarantees, proof, testimonials, case studies, track record, demonstrations.
Here's the key line: this is usually the CHEAPEST lever to move and the MOST neglected. A money-back guarantee costs you almost nothing but can double conversion. Remember this one — we come back to it constantly in the teardowns.
-->

---

<!-- _class: statement -->

# The cheapest lever to move.
### And the most neglected.

<!--
Let me make this its own moment because it's the single most actionable idea in the talk.
Perceived likelihood is the cheapest lever to move and the most neglected. A guarantee, a testimonial, a live demo — these cost almost nothing.
Yet most businesses pour everything into a bigger promise and forget to make anyone believe it. If you remember one lever from today, make it this one.
-->

---

<!-- _class: divider -->

### Lever 3 — push it down
# Time Delay ↓

<!--
How fast do they get the result?
Faster is always more valuable. Speed is a feature.
-->

---

## Time Delay ↓

How fast do they get the result? **Faster = more valuable.**

> "Lose weight in 90 days" beats "in 2 years."

<!--
Time delay is the gap between buying and getting the result. The shorter, the more valuable.
"Lose weight in 90 days" beats "lose weight in 2 years," even if the end state is identical. We discount the future heavily.
This is why "instant," "today," "in 5 minutes" are some of the most powerful words in marketing.
-->

---

<!-- _class: divider -->

### Lever 4 — push it down
# Effort & Sacrifice ↓

<!--
How much work, pain, or risk does the customer have to put in?
Less effort, less sacrifice, more value. Done-for-you beats do-it-yourself.
-->

---

## Effort & Sacrifice ↓

How much work, pain, or risk for the customer?

> **Done-for-you** beats do-it-yourself.

<!--
Effort and sacrifice is everything the customer has to give up — work, time, willpower, risk, money on the side.
Done-for-you beats done-with-you beats do-it-yourself. Every step you remove from the customer's plate raises value.
This is the entire premium-pricing logic of services: people pay enormous amounts to NOT have to do the work themselves.
-->

---

<!-- _class: statement -->

# Top up. Bottom down.

<!--
Quick recap before the big insight. Dream and Likelihood up. Time and Effort down.
Say it back to me: top up, bottom down. That's the mechanical instruction inside the fraction.
Now — here's where most businesses get it wrong.
-->

---

## The mistake almost everyone makes.

Most businesses compete **only on Dream Outcome.**

*A bigger promise. A bolder claim.*

<!--
Here's the most important insight in the whole talk. Most businesses compete on ONE lever: Dream Outcome.
They just keep making a bigger, bolder promise. "Even better results!" It's an arms race everyone is already in.
The problem: when everyone is shouting the same dream, the dream stops being a differentiator. You're now indistinguishable.
-->

---

<!-- _class: statement -->

# The leverage is in the **other three.**

<!--
The real edge lives in the three levers nobody else is touching.
Believability, speed, and ease. Perceived Likelihood, Time Delay, Effort & Sacrifice.
When your competitor is selling a slightly bigger dream and YOU sell the same dream but more believable, faster, and easier — you win. Every time.
-->

---

## Believability · Speed · Ease.

That's where you win.

<!--
Three words to carry out of this room: believability, speed, ease.
Believability = perceived likelihood. Speed = killing time delay. Ease = killing effort and sacrifice.
When you catch yourself reaching only for a bigger promise, stop and ask: can I instead make it more believable, faster, or easier? That's usually the cheaper, bigger win.
-->

---

### Part 2
# Live teardowns
*Score the four levers. Find the weak one.*

<!--
Now we make it real. I'll run four very different products through the equation out loud, with you.
For each: rate the four levers, find the weak one, name the fix, and identify the marketing pathway.
Then the recurring question — which lever is this company ACTUALLY selling? Often it's the wrong one.
-->

---

## Teardown 1 — SaaS

*A scheduling tool.*

- **Dream:** "never lose a booking"
- **Likelihood:** onboarding · integrations · social proof
- **Time delay:** kill setup friction
- **Effort:** automate it

<!--
First, a SaaS product — say a scheduling/booking tool. Dream Outcome: "you'll never lose a booking again."
The likelihood lever here is onboarding quality, integrations that prove it fits their stack, and social proof from similar businesses. Time delay is setup friction — the enemy; kill it. Effort is automation — the tool does the work.
Marketing pathway: free trial, ready-made templates, "set up in 5 minutes." Notice that pathway is selling SPEED and EASE, not a bigger dream.
-->

---

## Teardown 2 — AI / automation

*My lane.*

- **Dream:** "your tedious work disappears"
- The whole pitch = **Time Delay → 0** and **Effort → 0**

<!--
Second, an automation or AI service — this is my lane. Dream: "your tedious, repetitive work just disappears."
The entire pitch is collapsing Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice to near zero. That's WHY automation sells so well — it attacks the two bottom levers directly and hard.
Marketing pathway: before/after demonstrations, hours-saved numbers, an ROI calculator. You're literally quantifying the effort and time you removed.
-->

---

## Teardown 3 — Physical good

*A mattress or a supplement.*

- **Dream:** better sleep / better health
- **Likelihood:** reviews · trial period · money-back guarantee
- *100-night trial = removing the buyer's risk*

<!--
Third, a physical good — a mattress, a supplement. Dream: better sleep, better health.
The big lever is Perceived Likelihood, and it's moved by reviews, a trial period, and a money-back guarantee — that directly attacks perceived RISK. "Will this work for ME?" The guarantee answers it.
Marketing pathway: the 100-night trial. That's not a gimmick — it's removing effort and risk from the buyer and cranking believability. Cheapest lever, biggest move.
-->

---

## Teardown 4 — High-ticket service

*Consulting / coaching.*

- **Dream:** transformation
- **Done-for-you crushes Effort & Sacrifice**
- *...which is why it commands premium pricing*

<!--
Fourth, a high-ticket service — consulting or coaching. Dream: a real transformation.
Done-for-you crushes Effort & Sacrifice. The client doesn't have to figure it out, do the reps, or carry the risk alone — you do it with or for them.
That's precisely why these services command premium pricing: they obliterate the bottom-right lever. People pay the most to do the least.
-->

### Part 3
# What actually sells

<!--
The offer is half the battle. Now: pathways and which lever to lead with.
A great offer with no traffic fails; great traffic with a weak offer fails too.
-->

---

## Offer ≠ traffic.

- The **equation** builds the offer.
- The **four lead lanes** deliver the traffic.

<!--
A great offer fails with no traffic; great traffic fails with a weak offer. You need both.
The equation we've built today is the OFFER engine. The four lead lanes from the sales session — warm/cold × one-to-one/one-to-many — are the TRAFFIC engine.
Today is offer day. Just know the two halves have to meet.
-->

---

## Sell the lever they're most anxious about.

- Skeptical market → **likelihood** (proof, guarantee)
- Impatient market → **speed**
- Busy / lazy market → **ease** (done-for-you)
- Aspirational market → **dream**

<!--
Here's the rule for WHAT actually sells: lead with the lever the customer is most anxious about.
Skeptical market — they don't believe it works — sell likelihood: proof, guarantees. Impatient market — sell speed. Busy or overwhelmed market — sell ease, done-for-you. Aspirational market that already believes — then, and only then, sell the dream.
Diagnose the anxiety first. The right lever is the one that calms the specific fear in front of you.
-->

---

## Find your one growth lever.

*$100M Leads* + *Hacking Growth* (Sean Ellis)

> Instrument it. Double down. Don't diversify until it's saturated.

<!--
On the traffic side: $100M Leads plus Sean Ellis's Hacking Growth.
Find your ONE growth lever — the single channel or loop that's working — instrument it with data, and double down on it.
Don't diversify channels until that one is genuinely saturated. Beginners spread themselves across five channels and do all five badly.
-->

---

## Pigheaded discipline.

*The Ultimate Sales Machine* (Chet Holmes)

> Master a few fundamentals. Don't chase every new tactic.

<!--
Chet Holmes, The Ultimate Sales Machine. His phrase is "pigheaded discipline."
Pigheaded discipline on a few fundamentals beats chasing every shiny new tactic. The basics, done relentlessly, win.
This pairs with the equation: the four levers ARE the fundamentals. Master moving them and ignore the noise.
-->
